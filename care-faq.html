How do I treat a bee sting?

If the bee is a honey bee, then the stinger will remain in the skin and will need to be removed. This can be done with tweezers or being rubber off with a smooth object. Other bees will not leave their stinger in the skin. To help reduce the pain, ice can be applied and aspirin can be taken. If any sever reactions occur, such as nausea or vomitting, seek medical attention immediately.

Will any of your bees sting me?

All of our bees are solitary and docile. While the females are able to sting, they will only do so if provoked or threatened. The best way to stay friends with your bees is to avoid unnecesary large movement, such as swinging your arms around when near by them.

How do I help an injured bee?

If your bee is injured because of rain, bringing it to a dry place will help, but try to keep it outside so it can find some flower food once the rain has stoped. If you have found a sluggish bee you can offer it some plain granulated sugar water, with one sugar to every two water. Do not offer the bee honey or artificial sweetners.

Where is the best place to put my mason/leafcutter bee nest?

This depends on what you have in your backyard to work with. A tree that is sturdy enough to support the weight of your nest, that is not too close to your door, is ideal. However it may not necessarily be a tree, as a pole, shed, or wooden sidding is also an option. Just make sure it is at least 5 feet off the ground.

How do I remove an unwanted bee nest?

First, make sure you really want to remove this natural bee nest. Solitary bees are very docile, and most of them can't even sting. However, if they are being a burden then contacting a beekeeper as they are the most humane at removing bees.
