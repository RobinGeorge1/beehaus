General Bee Care

Not sure where to start, or if adopting bees is a good choice for you? Here we explain the general rules that apply to having any bees in your garden, and if adopting is good for you.

Your Garden

Flowers

The best way to ensure that your flowers are suitable and attractive for your bees is to plant ones native to your region. These flowers have been naturally supporting the local bees and will do wonderful for the ones you have adopted. Non-hybrid flowers, and flowers planted in large groups, are also great for bee attraction.

Lawn

Mowing your lawn less often will allow wildflowers to grow, this will help attract new bees and give a good source of nectar for you adopted bees.

What to be Wary of

Pesticides

Using pesticides in your garden can be very harmful to any bees, and typically only hinder your gardens growth in the long run. Most bugs are beneficial or neutral to your garden anyway, and water can even be sprayed on aphids to deter them.

Pets

Whether a pet is harmful to a bee, or bees are harmful to it, depends on the pet. All our adoptable bees only sting if provoked, and only the females can sting. If you have a very energetic or playful pet that has been stung before, precautions should be taken if you do decide to adopt some bees.

Pests: Pollen Mites

While many other bugs are beneficial to your garden as a whole, some are pests that can harm your bees. One such pests are pollen mites. They catch a ride on the adult bees, and once inside the nest with the larvae they will eat their food and possible even the larvae themselves. Protective inserts are recommended to hinder them.

Learn the Specifics On Our Adoptable Bees

Mason Bee

Named after the mud they use to line their homes, Mason bees are typically sought after by gardeners with fruit trees, but they are also very helpful with pollinating flowers. Solitary and non-aggresive, Mason bees are a wonderful addition to any garden.

Learn More

Leafcutter Bee

To line their nests, Leafcutter bees will take small, circular sections of leaves and overlap them within a small found cavity. No need to worry about the cuttings damaging the leafs or flowers, as their strong pollinating power more than makes up for it.

Learn More

Mining Bee

Do have sandy soil in your backyard? Then these bees would love to be adopted by you. As the name implies, Mining Bees reside underground, leaving a little mound on the surface. They are a lovely looking breed of bees, with the females being reddish orange.
