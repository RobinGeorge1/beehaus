Mining Bee

Do have sandy soil in your backyard? Then these bees would love to be adopted by you. As the name implies, mining bees reside underground, leaving a little mound on the surface. They are a lovely looking breed of bees, with the females being reddish orange.

Lifestyle

Life as a Solitary Bee

Mining bees are solitary bees, meaning instead of living in a hive with a queen like honey bees, every female mining bee is fertile and the sole provider for their eggs. That is not to say that they live alone, as mining bees are perfectly happy to live as neighbors in a home of hollow canes.

Super Pollinator

Mining bees are incredible pollinators, as their whole fuzzy body is used to collect the pollen from flowers instead of just special carriers on their hind legs like honey bees. Thanks to this gardens can flourish under their care.

Life Cycle

Early Spring

Place your cocoons in a shallow hole close by flowers in sandy soil. Mining bees nesting is further explained below in the Nest section.

Spring

The males will emerge first, and then a few weeks later so will the females to begin mating. The females will gather pollen + nectar, and lay their eggs in unused sections of the nest.

Late Spring

When the spring flowers are no longer blooming, the bees will go underground to hibernate with stores of food.

Winter

Now hibernating, they will not emerge until spring. Only active for 6-8 weeks of the year, they are a more reclusive bee species compaed to mason and leafcutter bees.

Nest

Your Mining Bees Home

With your adopted family of mining bees, a nest is not necessary to provide for them as they live underground. Simply burry the bee cocoons underground in a shallow hole in sandy soil close by to flowers. The female bees will burry their eggs in simple tunnels undergound, separting each egg with a wall of soil and food for when they hatch.

Flowers

Sunflowers

Tall and a lovely shade of bee attracting yellow, their a large and open faces are wonderful for bees. Sunflowers are a go to when it comes to giving your bees a rich supply of pollen.

Lavender

Lavender has a lovely fragance that both people and bees can enjoy. Just make sure they are planted in a sunny part of your garden.

Cosmos

These flat, open flowers give easy access to their pollen for bees. Plant in a large group to be even more attractive. They also have the bonus of being easy to take care of.

Poppies

Ideal for BC's moist climate, all Poppies are attractive to bees. This open flower is laden with pollen, and will provide quite the meal for your bees.

In our adoption process we first ask you about your region to make sure only bees that can thrive in your area are recommended. Due to this mining bees may not be suitable for you. Start the adoption process to figure out if mining bees are native to you!
